Arts & Culture

From unique neighborhood galleries such the innovative Museum of Design Atlanta (MODA) to our Grammy Award-winning Atlanta Symphony Orchestra, Atlanta's arts scene is waiting to be discovered. While visiting, check out some of the nation's most extraordinary exhibits, museums and theaters. And prepare to experience Atlanta's diverse art and culture!

Attractions

There's a lot to do here and it might even require a few extra days to see everything!. The must see Downtown Atlanta attractions like the World of Coca Cola, Georgia Aquarium, Inside CNN Atlanta and Imagine It!, and the Children's Museum of Atlanta, all circling Centennial Olympic Park, offer something for everyone.

Get back to nature at Zoo Atlanta, Fernbank Museum of Natural History and Stone Mountain Park. Or discover famous Atlantans at the Martin Luther King, Jr. National Historic Site, Margaret Mitchell House and Jimmy Carter Library & Museum

After Dark

When the sun sets, Atlanta nightlife starts heating up! No matter what style of nightlife you're craving, you're sure to find it in Atlanta. Explore the unique melting pot of fun giving you a choice of the laid-back vibes of Virginia Highland, the chic style in Buckhead, or a trendy scene in Midtown. Experience Atlanta after dark.

Neighborhoods

Atlanta has more than 70 streets that include the name "Peachtree," including the most well known, Peachtree Street. This main thoroughfare travels through the neighborhoods of Atlanta including Buckhead, Midtown and Downtown connecting you to numerous attractions, restaurants and shopping areas.

These neighborhoods boast an eclectic mix of arts, dining and a nightlife that's all the rage in places like Buckhead, Lenox Square and the Downtown convention and entertainment district offers even more!

Go where the locals go. Check out the stylish boutiques and laid-back bars in Virginia-Highland, live jazz in Castleberry Hill and cool alternative shopping, bars, restaurants and even an Elvis shrine at the Star Community Bar in Little Five Points. And then there's Cabbagetown, one of the emerging neighborhoods of Atlanta which Travel + Leisure magazine featured in "America's Best Secret Neighborhoods."
